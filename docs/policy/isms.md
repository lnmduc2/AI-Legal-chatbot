# INTER-K JSC - INFORMATION SECURITY REGULATIONS for EMPLOYEES

**Company Internal Use**

## PART I: INTRODUCTION

### 1. What is ISO 27001: 2013?
* ISO 27001:2013 is an international standard on Information Security Management System (ISMS), developed jointly by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and the International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC) and published on 1st October 2013.
* ISO 27001 spells out the requirements of an Information Security Management System, applicable to all organizations. 
* ISO 27001:2013 standard replaces its older edition (ISO 27001:2005).

### 2. What is ISMS?
* ISMS stands for INFORMATION SECURITY MANAGEMENT SYSTEM.
* It means the system to ensure Organizational information and information devices:
  * **SECURITY:** Protected from Loss, stolen, damaged or misuse.
  * **AVAILABILITY:** Always available for Staff to use at needed for Organization's business purposes.
* ISMS is consisted of: People, Process and Tools.

### 3. What is ISMS of Our Company?
Our Company established its ISMS, including:
* **PEOPLE**:
  * BOD direct and sponsor the ISMS.
  * ISM Manager to ensure ISMS to function effectively.
  * All Staff shall follow and improve ISMS.
* **PROCESS (Documents, Form...)**:
  * The document “Information Security Foundation”: Method based on ISO 27001:2013 and principle PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act).
  * The document “Information Security Policy”: the rules that all Staff, Suppliers and Customers shall follow.
  * The Standard, Process, Procedure, Guidelines / Work instruction to be followed in planning, monitoring, reporting checking, auditing, correcting, preventing, improving.
* **TOOLS**:
  * The Templates, forms to use to perform the Process.
  * The software tool (Example: Anti-virus, Network monitoring).

## PART II: REGULATIONS BASED ON POLICY

### I. Staff shall feedback to Managers about the context
* Every year (at least), BOD review the business context to define and update the ISMS.
* Business context includes: Customer requirements regarding Information Security, Law, the risks from external threats (such as virus, hacker, natural disaster, pandemic...and internal), and the opportunities such as new technologies.
* Updating ISMS includes: Policy, Process, Training to People, Tools.

### II. Staff shall follow IS Policy
* BOD establish the Information Security Policy.
* BOD provides direction, resources and support to the ISM Department and other Departments to ensure Information Security and Availability.
* ISM Manager communicates the Information Security Policy and any updates to all Staff.
* ISM Manager is responsible for Information Security Policy is followed.

### 1. Staff shall use Mobile devices properly
* Mobile devices include mobile phone, laptop, notebook, other mobile devices for input, process, output, storing of data.
* Mobile devices – except for mobile phones – brought into the Company workplace must be registered and approved by the IT Manager.
* Mobile devices that connect to the Company's network system and to other technology services must have device password.
* Follow Company IT department instruction of regulations on mobile devices.
* Using devices with network connectivity functions for creating or sharing WiFi to other devices for accessing Company network or Internet is strictly forbidden.
* Other network devices must be registered to the Company, tested and managed by the IT department, if any.

### 2. Staff shall follow Teleworking rules
* Teleworking and working from home are only allowed with ISM Manager’s approval with defined duration.
* The staff who works from home or teleworking is accountable for information security.

### III. Staff shall follow the Human Resource rules
* Staff/Contractor must sign and abide by Non-disclosure commitment along with his or her labor contract.
* Staff must attend Company’s information security training on the effective day of labor contract. Staff must attend Company’s information Security refresh training. Depending on the actual situation, the Board of Directors may decide the frequency of ISMS training, but at least once per year.
* Staff shall be aware of this policy and disciplinary process to take action against employees who have committed an information security breach.
* After termination from Company: Staff shall be aware that the responsibility of Information Security maintains after termination, according to the signed Non-Disclosure Agreement and Information Security Law.

### IV. Staff shall protect Company assets and use them properly

**Physical assets: Computers, Devices, Stationery**
* When see any risks, report immediately to Direct Manager.
* Only use physical assets after being assigned as PIC (Person in charge). Only use Company‘s assets work purposes.
* In temporary cases, Staff can use physical assets if permitted by PIC and with presence of PIC.
* PIC must protect assets from steal, loss or damage. In case of steal, loss or damage, Staff must report to the IS Manager or Direct Manager within 1 hour.
* Do not bring any Company's or customer's asset, confidential information out of the working area. Must have approval from ISM Manager when it is essential to bring out.
* Must have a proper encryption and preservation when taking any Company's or customers confidential information outside.
* Do not remove computers or technology device components, request IT department if installation or reparation is needed.
* Set up a five-minute automatic screensaver. Always lock the computer before leaving the desk or work areas.
* Do not share computers‘ local storage drives, files and folders over LAN/ Wi-Fi or using software.
* Do not share the same computer with others.
* Every unit must appoint a manager to manage public computers and devices.

**Information Assets: Documents, Design, DB schema, Source code …**
* Staff use Information with acceptable use purpose.
* Do not use internal information for personal purposes such as for job application or any tasks out of official assignment.
* Limit collection of personally identifiable information to the minimum necessary to accomplish the work purposes. Do not disclose personally identifiable information.
* Do not upload or store Company's confidential information into any public or personal website or web storage.
* Store important and confidential information in Company's file-servers or storage systems; do not store it in local computer‘s storage or mobile devices.
* When a project is closed, store project-related Information into project's file-servers or storage systems; do not store it in computers or mobile devices.
* Project-related information could be deleted from computers, emails and other storages, based on the requirements of the Company or project authorized person.
* The Company has full authority to monitor and log the access to Company's Information, data, computers, Information systems, networks, applications, software, email, websites and working areas.

**Customer’s lending assets**
* Customer supplied assets (infrastructure, information system, application/software, network connection, server, Internet, device, account/password, email...) are considered to be customer‘ properties. Follow customer's policy or work instruction on the usage of customer's assets when you are provided.
* Follow Company’s Information Security Regulation and IT Department’s guide for customers assets if the customer does not provide sufficient requirements or guidance.
* Use and preserve customers' devices, account/password, email, access right...which are assigned to you based on the “need to work” principle.
* It is illegal to hack or attack a customer's network or server; or use customer supplied assets to hack or attack external networks or servers.
* Hand-over customer's assets, if any, to person in charge when changing work position.

**Personal owned devices**
* Do not use personal owned devices (mobile devices, computers and network devices) for work purposes, unless it is essential to use those and have ISM Manager’s permission.
* Personal owned devices that connect to Company's information systems must be checked, tested and installed proper antivirus and endpoint control software by Company‘s IT department.

**CONFIDENTIALITY**
* 4 Levels: TOP CONFIDENTIAL, CONFIDENTIAL, INTERNAL USE, PUBLIC.
* By default, information is INTERNAL USE in Unit/Project.
* Do NOT access or share TOP CONFIDENTIAL information without BOD’s approval.
* Do NOT access or share CONFIDENTIAL information without permission of Author / Owner.
* Do NOT share Internal document outside of Unit/Project.
* Do NOT share COMPANY INTERNAL documentS outside of Company.

**MEDIA HANDLING**
* Removable media such as USB drive, removable disks… are prohibited.
* Do not use cameras, recorders or any devices of functions, at restricted areas. In case of essential need, must obtain ISMS Manager’s permissioning.
* Do not leave any confidential documents at public places such as printers; photocopy machines, meeting rooms….
* Printers are granted only to authorized staff. Do not use Company's printers for any unrelated work purposes.
* Users shall show evidence of deletion of data or disposal of media after use. When Destroy confidential hard copies, always use paper shredders.
* Media must be encrypted during transportation.
* Confidential information and documents must be transferred only by reliable and secure transport agents, except for emergency cases.
* Hard copies of confidential documents must be classified, placed and preserved in locked cabinets.
* During the information transfer process, always keep confidential information along with proper preservation methods.

### V. Access control
**Access of facilities**
* Always wear access card.
* Do NOT use other’s access card or let others use your card.
* Inform the Administration department if the access card is lost or omitted within two hours.
* Comply with security signs, safety and office signs at Company's premises. There are area signed as “Restricted Area”, where are restricted for only authorized persons.

**Access of networks and network services**
* Staff must keep his/her passwords totally secret. Do not lend or borrow Company accounts and password.
* Always follow the IT department’s policy of password creation and password change. Personal password must be confidential, which is not too simple to be searched or guessed.
* Always follow customer’s or third party’s regulation of password creation and password change when account and password are provided by customer or third party.
* Do not search or guess others‘ passwords.

### VI. Cryptography
* **Encryption:** Information classified as Top Confidential level shall be encrypted (set password) when being stored to hard disk or sent.
* **Key management:** When sending Key, Password: must sending separately with the Information, for example: in a separate email.

### VII. Physical and environmental security
**Secure areas**
* Do not let unauthorized persons come into the Company office without registration at Reception.
* Do not let unauthorized persons come into restricted areas without Manager’s permission.
* Staff must report to the Manager or Reception whenever they recognize a suspect.
* Do not let the confidential discussion (voice, slides, board, flipchart…) be hearable or seeable by outside. Ensure a clean desk and clean media after work.
* Staff must follow regulation defined for restricted areas.

**Equipment**
* Equipment must be placed at suitable places and protected from threats, hazards, or unauthorized access.
* Removal of assets - Equipment, information or software shall not be taken off-site without prior authorization.
* working outside the organization’s premises.
* Staff ensure that unattended equipment has appropriate protection.
* Staff ensure Clear desk and clear screen policy when leaving the workplace.

### VIII. Operations security
* Staff must ensure the malware protection installed, with right versions, as instructed by IT. Do not turn off or remove them.
* Computers and other information technology devices must be installed automatically with system and software updates.
* Disconnect Internet connection as soon as the computer is detected with a virus, report it to IT department for a solution.
* Intentionally spreading malicious programs (such as viruses, womis, trojans, bomb letters ...) is considered a Law violation.

### IX. Communication security
**Network Security Management**
* Access the information, which is approved by an authorized person of the Company or customer only.
* Do not use Intranet, information systems, servers, devices and the Internet for non-work purposes.
* Do not access websites that are unrelated to work purposes; do not use a gateway to bypass Company's or customer's proxy.
* Do not install servers that provide services such as Web, FTP, SVN, Proxy, Firewall, DC, DNS and DHCP without authorized person's permission.
* Do not configure network, do not set up Internet for computers that are in internal network without authorized person’s permission.
* Do not use online tools or public websites/storage to upload / translate documents containing Confidential information or Information provided by Customer.
* It is a severe law violation: hack or attack network or server.
* The use of cloud computing services for business purposes must be approved by the IT Department Manager. Refer to Cloud Computing Standard.

**Transferring information, Email**
* Do NOT use Company email for purposes unrelated to Company’s tasks. Non-work emails may be accepted with low frequency, not sent to the entire department or project team, with appropriate contents such as: team building, sharing knowledge.
* Do not use email to send or forward materials that could be considered as offensive or illegal.
* Always verify email addresses (To, Cc, Bcc), email subject, email contents, attached files and signature before sending an email.
* Do not use Company's email for testing; do not install email servers. If needed, Managers and IT permission is required.
* Do not use Company's emails to register to any forums or social websites.
* Do not open phishing emails or emails look suspicious.
* Do not disguise identity, and forge emails, crack passwords, hack, attack, or mass mailing to Company's or other email systems.
* Hand over your mailing lists and important emails, if any, to the person in charge when changing work position.

**Staff must follow the rules of using social media:**
* Do not post, upload, share or disclose internal / confidential information or photos of the Company or customer when participating in blogging and/or social media.
* Others’ personally identifiable information should not be made available on social media. Photos may be posted only with written permission of every single person in the photo.
* Do not post, upload, share or disclose any photos of customer sites or Company‘s ODC, restricted areas, “no camera” sign areas, on social media.

### X. Security incident management
* Staff must report to Manager any security incidents or security risks immediately to Manager or ISM Manager.
* Staff should contribute knowledge and experience to protect, fix, and prevent the incidents.

### XI. Discipline for violation
The staff that violates Information security regulation must be penalized one or all of these following disciplines:
* Bonus deductions.
* Participating in the ISMS re-training courses.
* Violation will be one of the factors to assess the level of task completion.
* Compensation for damage under the laws.
* Staff violations may also be considered according to disciplined behaviors and disciplined forms that are defined in Company's Labor regulations.

### XII. All other rules
* In case of Information security that are not listed in this Regulations, Staff must ask Manager or ISM Manager how to action.
